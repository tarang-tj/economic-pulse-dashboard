Key Findings

r=0.91
Fed Rate vs Inflation Lag
Correlation analysis reveals Fed Funds Rate movements precede CPI changes by approximately 9–12 months — consistent with monetary policy transmission theory.
−20M
COVID Payroll Shock
April 2020 saw the largest single-month payroll collapse in recorded US history. Full recovery to pre-pandemic levels took until mid-2022, roughly 26 months.
+1.2pp
WA Premium
Washington State unemployment tracked 1–1.5 percentage points above the national rate during the 2023–2024 tech sector downturn, driven by Seattle-area layoffs.
9.1%
40-Year CPI Peak
June 2022 CPI hit 9.1% YoY — the highest since 1981. The subsequent 18-month disinflation to ~3% tracked closely with Fed rate hikes, visible in the correlation matrix.
10M+
Job Openings Peak
JOLTS data peaked at over 12 million openings in early 2022 — nearly 2 openings per unemployed worker — before declining steadily as hiring cooled through 2023–24.
p<.10
Trend Detection Threshold
Short-term trends are flagged only when the 6-month rolling linear regression is both statistically significant (p<0.10) and explains at least 30% of variance (R²≥0.3).
03

ETL Pipeline

⬇
Stage 01
Extract
Pulls 7 macroeconomic time series from the FRED REST API with local 12-hour JSON caching to avoid redundant requests during development.
⚙
Stage 02
Transform
Cleans missing values, casts types, computes YoY changes distinguishing rate series (pp change) from index/level series (% change).
📐
Stage 03
Analyze
Detects short-term trends via scipy linear regression with significance gating. Builds a cross-indicator correlation matrix and flags NBER recession periods.
📊
Stage 04
Load
Renders an interactive Streamlit dashboard with Plotly time series, recession shading, rolling statistics, and a full correlation heatmap.
